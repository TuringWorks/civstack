Excellence Map 2.0 — Domain and Mission Levers

How outcome deficits and capability constraints become accountable CivStack work.

This companion to excellence-map.md replaces the original thirteen-axis lever catalogue. Outcomes, capabilities, and posture choices now play different roles:

  • an outcome deficit establishes why action matters;
  • a capability constraint identifies what prevents improvement;
  • a posture choice describes the route and its trade-offs;
  • a mission work order names what to build, who owns it, and how success will be verified.

Work-order template

Every routed intervention should contain:

  1. Outcome: which public result must change, for whom, by how much, and by when?
  2. Constraint: what evidence identifies this capability as binding?
  3. Posture: what strategic route was chosen, and which alternative was rejected?
  4. Intervention: which JTBD, process, asset, or role will change the constraint?
  5. Authority: who decides, who executes, who can stop the work, and who hears appeals?
  6. Resources: cost, workforce, data, infrastructure, dependencies, and transition burden.
  7. Measures: leading capability indicators, lagging outcomes, guardrails, and uncertainty.
  8. Review: cadence, trigger conditions, sunset or rollback path, and independent verification.

Prioritization rule

Do not rank work by numerical gap alone. Use an explicit rule such as:

priority = expected outcome impact × urgency × tractability × evidence confidence ÷ cost and downside risk

Publish the judgments behind each factor. A severe but weakly diagnosed gap should first generate a measurement or diagnostic mission, not a large deployment.

Closing rule

The Map succeeds only when it preserves this chain:

Outcome deficit → evidenced capability constraint → deliberate posture → accountable mission → verified public result

If the chain breaks, the work is activity without strategy—or measurement without consequence.
