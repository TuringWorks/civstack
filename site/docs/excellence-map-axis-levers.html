Excellence Map — Axis Levers

**How progress on each of the thirteen dimensions builds a better nation, and how adding jobs-to-be-done and roles, or optimizing the ones the library already has, moves each axis.**

This is the companion to excellence-map.md. The Map's gap engine tells you which axes to move toward a chosen destiny; this document tells you, for each axis, what work moves it — grounded in the real CivStack sectors, strategic missions, JTBD, and AI-personnel roles that exist in the library today.

First, what "progress on a posture axis" means

The Map's axes are bipolar postures, so "progress" is not "drive every axis to 100." It means three distinct, stackable things:

  • Positioning — moving to the value a chosen destiny needs (The Engine wants lean Slack; The Keystone wants redundant Slack — opposite positions, both legitimate).
  • Capability — operating better at whatever position you hold. A nation can be deliberately lean and still be excellent at being lean (fast, accurate, well-instrumented) rather than merely under-resourced. This is the kind of "progress" that is good regardless of destiny.
  • Quality gradient — a few axes do carry a direction that is broadly better for almost any destiny: Legitimacy toward verified/transparent, Ecological Posture toward regenerative, and the option value of Time Horizon toward long. These are marked below.

Each axis analysis names which kind of progress applies, then the levers.

The three levers the library gives you, per axis

  1. Add a JTBD — name a durable outcome the axis needs that no one currently owns, and stand up the team for it.
  2. Add a role — an AI-personnel agent, an embodied robot, an autonomous machine, or a human-command owner that executes part of that JTBD.
  3. Optimize / leverage an existing role — raise an existing role's quality, coverage, speed, or autonomy. This is the cheapest lever and usually the first to pull.

A structural note: adding or optimizing any AI/robot role inherently raises axis 10, Production Autonomy — so that axis is the meta-lever that every other axis's roles also push on. And every axis gain must be checked against the paired outcome-and-limits panel (HDI/IHDI, well-being, SDG dashboard, ecological overshoot): posture is a means, not an end.

The thirteen axes

1 · Time Horizon — Immediate ↔ Intergenerational · quality gradient (option value of long)

What progress buys. A longer horizon lets a nation invest in things that only pay off across decades — basic science, grid and water infrastructure, pension solvency, climate adaptation, sovereign savings — without being whipsawed by the electoral or quarterly cycle. The trade-off at the far pole is rigidity and under-responsiveness to the present; the win is the capability to hold long and short books at once.

Existing levers. OS 22 Resilience already owns the forward view through its Scenario generation agent, Dependency mapping agent, and Crisis dashboard analyst; OS 02 Public Finance carries the Budget scenario modeler and the Pension & retirement valuation agent. The JTBD "when financial risks emerge, forecast cash flow, debt, pensions, and macroeconomic exposure" is the seed.

Add or optimize. Add an intergenerational-impact JTBD ("when a decision binds future cohorts, price its long-run costs and benefits before signoff") owned by a new Intergenerational impact analyst and a Long-horizon capital steward (sovereign-wealth / strategic-fund allocation, archetype Investor/Steward). Optimize the existing Budget scenario modeler to run 30-year, not 3-year, horizons by default. Human-command owner: a strategist who is accountable for the long book.

2 · Frontier Posture — Consolidating ↔ Exploring · positional + capability

What progress buys. Exploration is how a nation captures new industries, defenses, and cures before rivals; consolidation is how it banks and scales what works. Moving toward the frontier raises the expected value of the future at the cost of more failed bets — so the real capability is cheap, fast, well-governed failure: many small experiments, fast kills, fast scale-ups.

Existing levers. OS 15 Science is the engine here — Hypothesis generator, Experiment planner, Patent landscape analyst, Reproducibility checker — composed by the Frontier AI Production, Quantum and Space Systems, and Science-to-Industry missions, with OS 12 for software-side experimentation.

Add or optimize. Add a frontier-bet-portfolio JTBD (ARPA-style: "when a high-risk/high-reward capability is plausible, fund a diversified portfolio of bets and kill or scale on evidence") with a Frontier-bet portfolio manager (human-command) and a Regulatory-sandbox design agent that lowers the cost of legally trying the unproven. Optimize the Reproducibility checker and add a Failure-postmortem & learning agent so dead bets feed the next round rather than vanishing.

3 · Coordination Mode — Emergent/market ↔ Directed/mission · positional

What progress buys. Directed coordination concentrates effort on a national objective (a chip fab, an energy build-out) that markets alone under-supply; emergent coordination harvests dispersed knowledge and avoids central error. Neither is "better" — the capability that matters is mobilizing a mission without crushing the market signals that keep it honest.

Existing levers. The strategic missions are this axis made concrete — each composes several sectors toward one objective. The human-command catalog supplies accountable owners (National technology strategist, Procurement innovation lead). OS 02's Procurement drafting agent and Grant compliance reviewer turn public demand into direction (procurement-as-policy); OS 01 Governance sets the rules.

Add or optimize. Add a mission-orchestration JTBD ("when a national objective spans sectors, sequence the sectors' jobs, budgets, and milestones into one plan") with a Mission-orchestration agent and an Industrial-policy design analyst (archetype Convener/Strategist). Optimize the existing Procurement drafting agent into a Demand-shaping agent that uses public purchasing to pull a strategic capability into existence (the Public Procurement for Frontier Technology mission).

4 · Openness — Sovereign/self-reliant ↔ Networked/interdependent · positional

What progress buys. Openness imports talent, capital, ideas, and cheaper inputs and projects influence outward; sovereignty reduces exposure to coercion and supply shocks. The capability is selective openness — deeply networked in what is safe and additive, self-reliant in what is strategically vulnerable.

Existing levers. OS 03 Defense & Foreign Affairs runs the interface — Translation agent, Sanctions-screening agent, OSINT analyst agent; OS 11 Logistics carries the Customs documentation agent; OS 16 Finance moves cross-border capital; the Strategic Supply Chain mission manages the dependency map.

Add or optimize. Add a talent-and-capital-attraction JTBD ("when the nation needs scarce skills or investment, find, attract, and settle them") with a Talent-attraction & visa-navigation agent and a Diaspora-engagement agent. Add a Trade-agreement analysis agent to OS 03. Optimize the Strategic Supply Chain dependency map into a live Chokepoint & substitution analyst so "openness" is continuously priced against concentration risk.

5 · Talent Formation — Broad/mass ↔ Deep/frontier-elite · positional + quality

What progress buys. Breadth gives universal capability and mobility; depth produces the few thousand frontier specialists who set the technological pace. A nation generally needs both, sequenced — broad foundations feeding deep peaks — so progress means widening the base and sharpening the tip at the same time.

Existing levers. OS 14 Education supplies Tutor agent, Lesson planner, Career pathway advisor, Training simulator, Accessibility adaptation agent; OS 20 Labor adds Skills inference agent and Workforce planning simulator; the Frontier Talent Formation mission composes 14 + 20 + 15 explicitly for the tip.

Add or optimize. Add a deep-specialist-pipeline JTBD ("when a frontier field needs experts faster than universities produce them, run accelerated apprenticeships in real labs") with a Research-talent scouting agent and an Apprenticeship-pathway designer. Optimize the Workforce planning simulator into a Skills-gap forecaster that tells Education which deep skills to build years ahead of demand. The Frontier Talent Formation mission is the integration point.

6 · Value Base — Extractive/physical ↔ Generative/knowledge · quality gradient (complexity)

What progress buys. Moving from rents on resources toward value created by knowledge, IP, and complex production raises economic complexity, wages, and resilience to commodity shocks. The risk is hollowing out the physical base; the capability is layering knowledge on top of the physical economy, not abandoning it.

Existing levers. OS 15 Science creates the knowledge; OS 12 Software turns it into product (Coding agent, Analytics agent); OS 09 Manufacturing's Digital twin simulation agent raises production sophistication; OS 08's Materials discovery agent upgrades inputs. The Science-to-Industry and Advanced Manufacturing missions are the bridge.

Add or optimize. Add a tech-transfer/commercialization JTBD ("when validated science exists, move it to pilot production, standards, procurement, and export") with an IP-commercialization agent and an Economic-complexity diversification planner that finds the adjacent, higher-complexity products a nation can credibly move into. Optimize OS 15's Patent landscape analyst to flag commercializable IP, not just map it.

7 · Slack / Antifragility — Lean/optimized ↔ Redundant/antifragile · positional

What progress buys. Slack — reserves, spare capacity, redundancy — is what turns a shock into an inconvenience instead of a catastrophe; leanness frees the resources slack ties up. The capability is buffering the few things that are existential (energy, water, food, medicine, payments) while staying lean elsewhere.

Existing levers. OS 22 Resilience owns it — Supply disruption monitor, Continuity plan reviewer, Dependency mapping agent; OS 07 Energy (Outage restoration planner), OS 06 Water (Asset maintenance planner), and OS 05 Food carry the sector buffers. OS 22 also owns the cross-sector keep-warm drills.

Add or optimize. Add a strategic-reserve-optimization JTBD ("for each existential input, hold the smallest reserve that survives the worst credible shock") with a Strategic-reserve optimization agent and a Redundancy / n+1 design analyst. Optimize the Supply disruption monitor into a continuous Stress-test & wargaming agent (reusing the world-models from _catalogs/simulation-training/) so buffers are sized against simulated, not historical, shocks.

8 · Optimand — Output/achievement ↔ Flourishing/wellbeing · positional, with an outcome tie

What progress buys. Steering toward flourishing puts health, time, security, and life-satisfaction alongside GDP in the objective function; steering toward output maximizes measured production. Because this axis decides what the nation is for, it is the one most tightly coupled to the outcome panel — moving it should show up directly in HDI, well-being, and equity.

Existing levers. OS 21 Household & Care (Care coordination agent, Benefits navigator, Social services referral agent), OS 13 Healthcare (Care gap analyst, Public health surveillance agent), OS 18 Media & Civic Life (social cohesion, Misinformation monitoring agent), and OS 14 Education together carry the lived-wellbeing JTBD.

Add or optimize. Add a wellbeing-budgeting JTBD ("when budgets are set, score options on a wellbeing frame, not output alone" — the New Zealand / Bhutan model) with a Wellbeing-budgeting agent and a Time-use & quality-of-life analyst. Add a Social-connection / loneliness-program agent (OS 21 + OS 18) to target horizontal cohesion. Optimize the Benefits navigator for take-up and dignity, not just eligibility.

9 · Decision Tempo — Deliberative/consensus ↔ Decisive/agile · positional + capability

What progress buys. Faster, more concentrated decisions ship infrastructure, permits, and emergency responses in months not decades; deliberation builds legitimacy and catches error. The capability — and the safeguard — is speed without losing the audit trail and appeal rights; tempo must never be bought by hollowing out Legitimacy (axis 12).

Existing levers. OS 04 Public Safety runs at real-time tempo (Dispatch optimizer, Incident summarization agent, Resource allocation agent); OS 01 Governance carries the deliberative machinery (Public comment analyzer, Records and transparency agent); OS 10 Shelter's Permitting assistant and Code compliance checker are where slow tempo bites hardest in the real economy.

Add or optimize. Add a permitting-acceleration JTBD ("when a project is lawful, clear it in the shortest defensible time") with a Permitting-acceleration agent and a Regulatory-cycle-time analyst that measures and attacks decision latency. Pair every tempo gain with an After-action / decision-log agent so faster decisions stay reviewable — keeping axis 9 and axis 12 moving together.

10 · Production Autonomy — Hand/human-laborious ↔ Autonomous/machine-run · capability (the meta-lever)

What progress buys. Running more work on the human + AI + robot stack lifts output per worker, takes humans out of dull/dirty/dangerous tasks, and — crucially — is how every other axis's new roles get staffed without proportional headcount. The risk is deskilling and brittle over-automation; the safeguards are the verified deterministic safety layer and the keep-warm simulators.

Existing levers. This is the whole CivStack stack: _catalogs/embodied-ai-stack/ (brain, VLA policy, world-model, safety), _catalogs/autonomous-fleet-ops/, _catalogs/capability-optimization/ (the method/model router), plus every sector's roles/, robots/, and autonomous/ folders. The tools/capability-router.html already operationalizes "route each capability to the cheapest safe tier."

Add or optimize. The highest-value move here is optimization, not addition: push each existing AI role up the capability ladder where it is safe to do so, using the capability router, and retire hand-work JTBD into autonomous ones. Pair every increment with OS 22's keep-warm drills and _catalogs/simulation-training/ so the human fallback bench stays current, and an OS 20 Labor-transition agent so displaced workers move into the new roles the other axes create.

11 · Physical Throughput — Constrained/scarce ↔ Abundant/surplus · quality gradient (headroom)

What progress buys. Energy and material headroom is the substrate every other ambition draws on — compute for AI, desalination for water, electrification for industry, propulsion for space. Surplus is permissive; scarcity forces zero-sum trade-offs. The trade-off to manage is throughput vs Ecological Posture (axis 13): abundance must be clean to be sustainable.

Existing levers. The Energy Abundance mission composes OS 07 Energy (Load forecasting agent, Energy market analyst, Outage restoration planner, Permitting documentation agent) with OS 08 Mining and OS 06 Water. The JTBD "when decarbonization is required, integrate renewables, storage, demand response, nuclear, hydro, geothermal, and efficiency" is the seed.

Add or optimize. Add a generation-buildout JTBD ("when demand will outgrow supply, plan and permit clean capacity ahead of the curve") with a Generation-buildout planning agent and a Grid-interconnection-queue accelerator (the queue is the real bottleneck in many grids). Optimize OS 07's Load forecasting agent with a Demand-response optimizer so existing capacity stretches further. Keep this axis explicitly coupled to axis 13.

12 · Legitimacy Base — Inherited/institutional ↔ Verified/transparent · quality gradient (trust)

What progress buys. Anchoring trust in things that can be checked — auditable records, transparent algorithms, working appeal and redress — makes a state more credible at home and abroad, lowers corruption, and is the precondition for every other axis being trusted to move. This is the axis behind The Lodestar destiny and the strongest single quality gradient on the wheel.

Existing levers. OS 23 Identity & DPI is the backbone — Identity verification agent, Consent & data-exchange agent, Grievance & redress agent, Inclusion-gap analyst; OS 01 Governance's Records and transparency agent; OS 18's Misinformation monitoring agent; the Digital Infrastructure mission ties the rails together.

Add or optimize. Add an algorithmic-accountability JTBD ("when a public decision is automated, make it explainable, auditable, and appealable") with an Algorithmic-transparency & audit agent and a Public-explainability agent that renders decisions in plain language. Add a Participatory-consultation agent (OS 01 + OS 18) for legitimacy-by-voice. Optimize the Grievance & redress agent so appeal is fast and real, not nominal — appeal latency is itself a legitimacy metric.

13 · Ecological Posture — Extractive/overshoot ↔ Regenerative/within-limits · quality gradient (limits)

What progress buys. Staying within ecological limits protects the natural base every future depends on and is increasingly a condition of market access and legitimacy. The tension is with short-run output and throughput; the capability is decoupling — growing value while shrinking material and carbon footprint.

Existing levers. OS 19 Environment owns it — Emissions accounting agent, Satellite monitoring analyst, Climate risk modeler, Waste stream optimization agent, Permit compliance agent; OS 08 Mining's "recycle critical materials" JTBD; OS 05 Food's stewardship; the Bioeconomy mission for biological production within limits.

Add or optimize. Add a circular-economy JTBD ("when materials reach end-of-life, recover and recirculate them so virgin extraction falls") with a Circular-economy / material-flow agent and a Natural-capital accounting agent that books ecosystems as assets on the national balance sheet. Add a Regenerative-land & ecosystem-restoration planner (OS 19 + OS 05). Optimize the Emissions accounting agent into real-time, decision-grade footprint data so axis 11 and axis 13 can be traded off live.

How this closes the loop

The Excellence Map's gap engine names which axes a chosen destiny most needs you to move. This document gives each of those axes a concrete work order in CivStack's own terms: the JTBD to stand up, the AI/robot/human-command roles to add, and the existing roles to optimize first. Three rules hold across all thirteen:

  1. Optimize before you add. Leveling up an existing role is cheaper and faster than a new JTBD; reach for new roles when no existing one owns the outcome.
  2. Production Autonomy is the multiplier. Every new or optimized AI/robot role also lifts axis 10 — which is how a nation staffs ambition on all the other axes without proportional headcount, provided the keep-warm and safety layers hold.
  3. Validate on the outcome panel. Every axis gain is a means; confirm it shows up as better lives and a healthier ecological balance (HDI/IHDI, well-being, SDG dashboard, overshoot) before calling it progress.
