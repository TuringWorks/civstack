Frontier Technology Preeminence — Strategy Note

The strategic thesis behind the strategic missions. Imported/adapted from Agentic-Workforce. Where the 22 operating systems answer "what work must a society do," and the strategic missions answer "what national capabilities must it compose," this note answers "why, and in what order."

Thesis

A preeminent technology nation needs a full-stack national capability, not only research talent. The stack:

  1. Frontier discovery
  2. Compute and data infrastructure
  3. Semiconductor and hardware sovereignty
  4. AI model production and evaluation
  5. Robotics and embodied automation
  6. Advanced manufacturing and materials
  7. Energy abundance and grid resilience
  8. Bioeconomy, quantum, space, and cyber capability
  9. Standards, safety, regulation, procurement, and exports
  10. Talent formation, capital allocation, and public trust

Each layer maps to one or more CivStack strategic missions and pulls roles from several of the 22 operating systems.

Sequencing

The realistic order is not "do everything": compute, energy, talent, and supply-chain resilience are enabling layers that gate the rest, so they tend to come first; semiconductor, AI-model, and robotics capability build on them; bioeconomy, quantum, and space are higher-leverage but later. Every nation sets this order differently by scale, income, resource endowment, and geopolitics (sovereign build vs ally-and-buy vs import) — see each mission's context modifiers and the library's transition dynamics section.
