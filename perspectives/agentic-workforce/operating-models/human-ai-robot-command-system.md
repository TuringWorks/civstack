# Human-AI-Robot Command System

Purpose: define how accountable humans, AI personnel, and humanoid/specialized robots coordinate inside a national or institutional workforce.

## Core Principle

Humans own legitimacy, goals, values, safety, exceptions, relationships, and final signoff. AI personnel accelerate cognitive, digital, analytical, drafting, monitoring, and coordination work. Robots execute bounded physical tasks in mapped environments. The command system exists to make delegation fast without losing accountability.

## Three-Layer Workforce

| Layer | Primary function | Examples | Must never lose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Human command | Accountable judgment, authority, trust, ethics, relationships | minister, commander, lab director, plant manager, clinician, regulator | ownership, legitimacy, escalation, redress |
| AI personnel | Research, drafting, coding, monitoring, simulation, evaluation, coordination | research agent, coding agent, policy simulator, SOC agent, procurement agent | evidence, uncertainty, constraints, logs |
| Robot personnel | Physical execution, inspection, logistics, maintenance support | lab robot, warehouse robot, inspection walker, care support robot | safety envelope, human override, physical proof |

## Operating Loop

1. Mission assignment: human owner defines objective, constraints, success criteria, and risk tier.
2. Context loading: agents load approved data, policies, tools, maps, and current state.
3. Task decomposition: AI personnel separate research, planning, execution, monitoring, and verification.
4. Delegation: AI handles cognitive work; robots handle approved physical work; humans handle judgment and exceptions.
5. Verification: outputs are checked against metrics, evidence, tests, inspections, and human review thresholds.
6. Escalation: uncertainty, rights impact, safety risk, conflict, or policy ambiguity triggers human command.
7. Learning: incidents, failures, and successful patterns update SOPs, evals, prompts, maps, and training.

## Delegation Rules

- Delegate to AI when the work is text, code, data, classification, monitoring, forecasting, simulation, routing, or first-draft synthesis.
- Delegate to robots when the work is fetch, carry, inspect, clean, sort, stage, load, unload, scan, guide, or repeatable manipulation in a bounded environment.
- Keep with humans when the work involves force, rights, consent, accountability, public legitimacy, final professional signoff, scarce-resource triage, or unresolved ethical tradeoffs.

## Required Control Surfaces

- Role charter: mission, owner, scope, permissions, prohibited actions.
- Context pack: policies, data sources, standards, SOPs, examples, escalation rules.
- Tool permissions: what each AI/robot can read, write, trigger, buy, move, message, or execute.
- Evidence log: sources, actions, decisions, assumptions, model/robot version, timestamps.
- Evaluation: quality, safety, robustness, bias, latency, cost, and user impact.
- Incident path: stop conditions, escalation contacts, rollback, disclosure, redress.
- Review cadence: daily operations, weekly failure review, quarterly risk review, annual redesign.

## Command Cadence

- Real-time: safety events, incidents, outages, cyber alerts, medical/public safety escalations.
- Daily: queues, uptime, throughput, blocked work, robot incidents, AI exceptions.
- Weekly: metrics, quality drift, cost, adoption, workforce impact.
- Monthly: risk register, eval results, audit trails, policy updates.
- Quarterly: role redesign, procurement, capacity, training, public trust, resilience.

## Failure Modes

- Automation without accountable owner.
- AI output treated as final decision.
- Robot task envelope expands informally.
- Hidden data drift or model drift.
- Poor escalation and unreviewed exceptions.
- Workers bypassing controls because tools slow them down.
- Public harm without appeal or redress.
- Over-optimization of local metrics against national mission.

## Minimum Readiness Checklist

- Human owner named.
- Risk tier assigned.
- Permissions documented.
- Context pack available.
- Eval criteria defined.
- Escalation path tested.
- Incident logging enabled.
- Affected users/workers trained.
- Shutdown/rollback path tested.
- Metrics reviewed by accountable humans.
