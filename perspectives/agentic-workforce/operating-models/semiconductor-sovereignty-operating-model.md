# Semiconductor Sovereignty Operating Model

Purpose: coordinate chip design, fabrication, packaging, equipment, materials, workforce, security, and strategic demand.

## Mission

Ensure access to critical chips and semiconductor capabilities for AI, defense, telecom, energy, automotive, healthcare, and industrial systems.

## Core Capabilities

- Chip architecture and EDA.
- Process technology and wafer fabrication.
- Advanced packaging.
- Equipment maintenance and spare parts.
- Specialty gases, chemicals, wafers, masks, and critical materials.
- Yield engineering and quality.
- Trusted supply chain and export-control compliance.
- Workforce training and cleanroom operations.

## Human Command Roles

- Semiconductor fab director.
- Chip architecture lead.
- Yield engineering lead.
- Process integration lead.
- Advanced packaging lead.
- Semiconductor supply-chain lead.
- Fab EHS/security lead.
- Workforce training lead.

## AI Personnel

- EDA copilot.
- Design rule checker.
- Yield analysis agent.
- Process anomaly detector.
- Equipment maintenance predictor.
- Supplier risk agent.
- Production scheduler.
- Export-control screening agent.

## Robot Personnel

- Cleanroom logistics robot.
- Inspection walker robot.
- Maintenance tool runner.
- Inventory scanning robot.

## Operating Loop

1. Identify strategic chip demand and priority customers.
2. Align design, process, packaging, and supply-chain readiness.
3. Schedule production capacity and qualify materials/equipment.
4. Monitor yield, defects, tool health, contamination, and delivery.
5. Diagnose process variation and implement controlled changes.
6. Maintain EHS, security, and export-control compliance.
7. Feed lessons into design, process, workforce, and supplier strategy.

## Required Artifacts

- Strategic chip demand map.
- Process flow and control plan.
- Yield dashboard.
- Supplier dependency map.
- Tool uptime dashboard.
- Cleanroom safety and contamination plan.
- Export-control compliance record.
- Workforce training matrix.

## Metrics

- Yield.
- Defect density.
- Cycle time.
- Tool uptime.
- Supplier resilience.
- Strategic demand fulfilled.
- Safety incidents.
- Export-control violations.
