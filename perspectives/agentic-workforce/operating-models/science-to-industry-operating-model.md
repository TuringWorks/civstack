# Science-To-Industry Operating Model

Purpose: move frontier science from discovery to validated technology, pilot production, standards, procurement, and exports.

## Mission

Shorten the path from research insight to national capability.

## Core Capabilities

- Frontier research programs.
- Reproducibility and metrology.
- Prototype engineering.
- Testbeds and pilot lines.
- Standards development.
- Technology transfer and IP.
- Procurement pathways.
- Venture and growth capital.
- Workforce training.

## Human Command Roles

- Frontier lab director.
- Principal investigator.
- Prototype engineering lead.
- Standards diplomat.
- Technology transfer officer.
- Procurement innovation lead.
- Industrialization lead.
- Research ethics and safety officer.

## AI Personnel

- Literature review agent.
- Hypothesis generator.
- Experiment planner.
- Simulation agent.
- Lab data analyst.
- Patent landscape analyst.
- Standards comparison agent.
- Grant/proposal drafting agent.

## Robot Personnel

- Lab assistant robot.
- Field measurement robot.
- Prototype assembly robot.
- Hazardous-material support robot.

## Operating Loop

1. Define frontier problem and success criteria.
2. Review evidence and generate hypotheses.
3. Run experiments with reproducible protocols.
4. Build prototypes and testbeds.
5. Validate performance, safety, and economic feasibility.
6. Protect IP and contribute to standards.
7. Use procurement, pilots, and capital to scale.
8. Feed production and deployment learning back into research.

## Required Artifacts

- Research roadmap.
- Experiment registry.
- Data and protocol repository.
- Reproducibility checklist.
- Prototype test results.
- IP/standards map.
- Pilot deployment plan.
- Safety/ethics review.

## Metrics

- Time from hypothesis to validated result.
- Reproducibility rate.
- Prototype performance.
- Pilot adoption.
- Patent/standards output.
- Technology transfer revenue or impact.
- Safety incidents.
