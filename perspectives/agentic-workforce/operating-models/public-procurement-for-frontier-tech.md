# Public Procurement For Frontier Technology

Purpose: use public demand to accelerate strategic technology deployment while preserving competition, transparency, safety, and accountability.

## Mission

Turn national needs into early markets for high-value technologies.

## Core Capabilities

- Mission-driven requirements.
- Challenge procurement.
- Testbeds and pilots.
- Outcome-based contracts.
- Safety and evaluation gates.
- Vendor risk management.
- Domestic/allied supply-chain requirements.
- Scale-up pathways after successful pilots.

## Human Command Roles

- Procurement innovation lead.
- Technical buyer.
- Mission owner.
- Evaluation lead.
- Vendor risk lead.
- Contracting officer.
- Public accountability officer.

## AI Personnel

- Procurement agent.
- Market intelligence agent.
- RFP drafting agent.
- Vendor risk agent.
- Evaluation evidence agent.
- Contract obligation tracker.
- Cost-benefit simulator.

## Robot Personnel

- Receiving/inventory robot.
- Testbed support robot.
- Inspection robot where physical pilots are involved.

## Operating Loop

1. Define mission outcome and constraints.
2. Survey market and technology readiness.
3. Draft requirements around outcomes and evaluation evidence.
4. Run transparent competition or challenge.
5. Pilot with safety, privacy, and performance gates.
6. Scale successful vendors and terminate weak pilots.
7. Feed lessons into standards, budgets, and future procurement.

## Required Artifacts

- Mission need statement.
- Market scan.
- RFP/challenge brief.
- Evaluation rubric.
- Pilot safety plan.
- Vendor risk assessment.
- Contract terms.
- Scale decision memo.

## Metrics

- Procurement cycle time.
- Pilot success rate.
- Cost per outcome.
- Vendor diversity and resilience.
- Safety findings.
- Scale-up speed.
- Public value delivered.
