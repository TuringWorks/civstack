# Frontier Talent Formation Operating Model

Purpose: build the human talent system needed to lead frontier technology while using AI tutors, simulation, labs, apprenticeships, and robot-enabled facilities.

## Mission

Continuously produce, reskill, and retain people capable of leading, supervising, and improving AI- and robot-augmented systems.

## Core Capabilities

- Foundational education in literacy, numeracy, science, computing, civic judgment, and collaboration.
- Technical pathways for AI, robotics, chips, energy, biotech, quantum, cyber, manufacturing, and logistics.
- Apprenticeships and lab/factory-based training.
- AI tutoring and personalized practice.
- Credentialing and skills verification.
- Worker transition and job redesign.
- Immigration and talent attraction where applicable.

## Human Command Roles

- National talent formation lead.
- Curriculum architect.
- Apprenticeship director.
- Frontier university/lab partnership lead.
- Workforce transition lead.
- Credentialing and assessment lead.
- AI tutor governance lead.

## AI Personnel

- AI tutor.
- Curriculum alignment agent.
- Skills inference agent.
- Career pathway advisor.
- Assessment generation agent.
- Labor-market demand analyst.
- Training simulator agent.

## Robot Personnel

- Classroom/lab assistant robot.
- Vocational demonstration robot.
- Facilities maintainer robot.
- Lab assistant robot.

## Operating Loop

1. Forecast strategic workforce demand by sector and skill.
2. Map current education and labor supply.
3. Design modular curricula and credentials.
4. Deploy AI tutors and simulation practice.
5. Connect learners to labs, factories, internships, and apprenticeships.
6. Verify skills through performance-based assessment.
7. Place workers into roles and update training from job performance data.

## Required Artifacts

- Strategic skills taxonomy.
- Curriculum maps.
- Credential rubrics.
- AI tutor safety policy.
- Apprenticeship standards.
- Lab/factory training inventory.
- Labor-market dashboard.
- Worker transition plan.

## Metrics

- Time to competence.
- Credential completion.
- Job placement.
- Employer satisfaction.
- Skill retention.
- Transition success.
- Equity of access.
