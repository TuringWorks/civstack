# Frontier Technology Preeminence Workforce Strategy

Purpose: define the jobs and roles needed for a country to lead the next technology wave while delegating execution to AI personnel and humanoid robots wherever appropriate.

## Strategic Thesis

A preeminent technology country needs a full-stack national capability, not only research talent. The stack is:

1. Frontier discovery.
2. Compute and data infrastructure.
3. Semiconductor and hardware sovereignty.
4. AI model production and evaluation.
5. Robotics and embodied automation.
6. Advanced manufacturing and materials.
7. Energy abundance and grid resilience.
8. Bioeconomy, quantum, space, and cyber capability.
9. Standards, safety, regulation, procurement, and exports.
10. Talent formation, capital allocation, and public trust.

## Operating Model

- Human leaders set national missions, risk appetite, public legitimacy, and final signoff.
- AI personnel accelerate research, planning, design, coding, evaluation, documentation, procurement, monitoring, and coordination.
- Humanoid robots execute physical work in human-built environments: labs, fabs, plants, hospitals, warehouses, farms, data centers, ports, and disaster zones.
- Standards bodies convert domestic capability into global influence.
- Public procurement creates early demand for critical technologies.
- Export controls and trade policy protect strategic advantage without isolating the innovation ecosystem.

## Non-Negotiable Controls

- Every AI/robot role must have a named human owner.
- Every high-consequence workflow needs logs, evals, escalation rules, rollback, and appeal.
- Every frontier model or robot fleet needs red-team evaluation before deployment.
- Every national technology program needs supply-chain, cyber, energy, labor, and public-trust plans.
- No autonomous final authority for force, detention, rights denial, clinical decisions, grid emergencies, nuclear operations, or democratic decisions.

## Build Sequence

1. Inventory current national capability by domain.
2. Define frontier missions and measurable milestones.
3. Create human command roles for each mission.
4. Assign AI personnel to research, simulation, coding, evaluation, procurement, and operations.
5. Assign robot personnel to lab, factory, logistics, inspection, and maintenance tasks.
6. Build shared compute, data, eval, procurement, and standards infrastructure.
7. Create fast procurement and commercialization pathways.
8. Train citizens through AI tutors, apprenticeships, labs, and robot-enabled facilities.
9. Export products, platforms, standards, and operating models.
10. Continuously audit safety, concentration risk, workforce transition, and public legitimacy.
