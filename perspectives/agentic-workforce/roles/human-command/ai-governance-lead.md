# AI Governance Lead

## Mission

Ensure AI systems are useful, safe, lawful, auditable, equitable, secure, and aligned with institutional values.

## When Needed

- AI systems influence decisions, products, citizens, customers, workers, or infrastructure.
- Models are deployed into regulated, safety-critical, or rights-impacting environments.
- AI incidents, drift, bias, or security risks must be managed.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When AI is proposed, classify risk so the right controls apply.
- When models are built, require evaluations so quality and safety are measured before deployment.
- When AI affects people, preserve appeal, transparency, privacy, and redress.
- When models drift or fail, coordinate incident response and remediation.

## Responsibilities

- Maintain AI inventory, risk tiers, evaluation standards, approval gates, incident playbooks, and audit trails.
- Coordinate legal, security, privacy, product, engineering, compliance, and affected communities.
- Define prohibited uses and human-review requirements.

## AI Delegation

- Model evaluation agent, audit agent, policy comparison agent, incident analysis agent, red-team planning agent.

## Robot Delegation

- Evidence collection and physical inspection support where AI is embodied in robot fleets.

## Human Boundary

Final deployment approval, risk acceptance, incident disclosure, rights-impacting appeals, and public accountability remain human-led.

## Metrics

- Model inventory completeness.
- Evaluation coverage.
- Incident rate/severity.
- Time to remediation.
- Appeal outcomes.
- Bias and robustness performance.
- Audit readiness.

## Context Needed By Agents

Model cards, eval suites, incident logs, policies, legal requirements, data lineage, user impact assessments, security reviews, and release history.
