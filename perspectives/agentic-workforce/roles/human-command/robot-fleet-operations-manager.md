# Robot Fleet Operations Manager

## Mission

Operate humanoid and specialized robot fleets safely, productively, and acceptably in human environments.

## When Needed

- Robots perform recurring physical work.
- Multiple sites, shifts, or robot models require coordination.
- Safety, uptime, maintenance, training, and human acceptance matter.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When robot work is introduced, define the task envelope so robots do useful work without unsafe autonomy.
- When robots fail or escalate, resolve issues and improve procedures.
- When humans and robots share space, maintain trust, signage, training, and incident response.
- When fleet data accumulates, improve routing, maintenance, task design, and ROI.

## Responsibilities

- Define robot roles, safety envelopes, escalation rules, maintenance routines, site maps, and human override.
- Coordinate facilities, safety, IT, legal, labor, vendors, and operations.
- Monitor uptime, task completion, damage, near misses, and human feedback.

## AI Delegation

- Fleet scheduling agent, maintenance prediction agent, incident summarization agent, route planner, training-data curator.

## Robot Delegation

- Robot fleet executes approved physical tasks: fetch, carry, inspect, clean, sort, assemble, guide, and assist.

## Human Boundary

Safety certification, task authorization, incident accountability, worker relations, and public-facing deployment decisions remain human-owned.

## Metrics

- Uptime.
- Task completion.
- Escalation quality.
- Near misses.
- Damage rate.
- Human satisfaction.
- Cost per completed task.

## Context Needed By Agents

Site maps, task library, safety rules, robot capabilities, maintenance logs, incident reports, labor agreements, access controls, and escalation contacts.
