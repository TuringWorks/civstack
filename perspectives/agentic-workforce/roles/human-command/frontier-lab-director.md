# Frontier Lab Director

## Mission

Convert scientific uncertainty into validated frontier capability through disciplined research programs, talent, infrastructure, safety, and technology transfer.

## When Needed

- A national mission depends on new science or engineering capability.
- Research must move from papers to prototypes to deployment.
- Dual-use, safety, reproducibility, and capital intensity must be managed.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When a research frontier is unclear, shape questions into programs so teams can make measurable progress.
- When experiments produce signals, separate noise from discovery so resources move toward truth.
- When discoveries mature, connect them to manufacturing, procurement, standards, and commercialization.
- When research creates risk, govern ethics, security, and dual-use implications.

## Responsibilities

- Set research agenda, milestones, staffing, equipment, data practices, publication strategy, and safety review.
- Allocate lab resources across exploratory, translational, and platform work.
- Coordinate universities, startups, industry, government, and standards bodies.

## AI Delegation

- Literature review, hypothesis generation, experiment planning, simulation, lab data analysis, grant drafting, patent landscape review.

## Robot Delegation

- Sample handling, equipment loading, lab logistics, repetitive measurement, hazardous handling under approved protocols.

## Human Boundary

Research ethics, scientific claims, publication, IP strategy, dual-use release, hiring, and funding priorities remain human-led.

## Metrics

- Validated breakthroughs.
- Reproducibility.
- Prototype cycle time.
- Publications/patents/standards contributions.
- Translation to products or public capability.
- Safety incidents.

## Context Needed By Agents

Research roadmap, lab capabilities, safety protocols, data schemas, literature, experimental history, funding constraints, IP policy, and commercialization pathway.
