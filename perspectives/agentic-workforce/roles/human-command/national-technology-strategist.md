# National Technology Strategist

## Mission

Set national direction for frontier technology capability and align research, industry, defense, education, capital, standards, exports, and public legitimacy.

## When Needed

- A country wants technology preeminence.
- Critical technology supply chains are fragile.
- Frontier systems create strategic or social risk.
- Public and private investments need coordination.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When technology waves emerge, identify national missions so scarce capital and talent concentrate on high-leverage bets.
- When industries fragment, coordinate public, private, university, and defense capabilities so the country can move as a system.
- When global competition intensifies, protect strategic advantage while keeping innovation open enough to compound.
- When automation changes labor, redesign education and workforce systems so people move upward instead of being stranded.

## Responsibilities

- Sense: monitor science, markets, standards, competitors, supply chains, talent, and public sentiment.
- Interpret: distinguish hype from durable capability and map second-order effects.
- Decide: set priorities, funding themes, risk appetite, and institutional ownership.
- Mobilize: align procurement, grants, regulation, immigration, education, and industry partnerships.
- Verify: track milestones, capability gaps, adoption, safety, and export strength.
- Govern: preserve legitimacy, competition, privacy, safety, and national resilience.

## AI Delegation

- Policy simulator, research analyst, dependency mapping agent, market intelligence agent, standards comparison agent.
- Use AI to draft option memos, scenario maps, budget comparisons, and capability scorecards.

## Robot Delegation

- Low direct fit. Use robot evidence from factories, labs, logistics hubs, and infrastructure inspections.

## Human Boundary

National priorities, public tradeoffs, emergency powers, military implications, rights impacts, and legitimacy remain human accountable.

## Metrics

- Frontier capability milestones.
- Domestic manufacturing share for strategic inputs.
- Time from research to deployment.
- Standards influence.
- Export growth in strategic sectors.
- Workforce transition success.
- Resilience under supply shocks.

## Context Needed By Agents

National strategy documents, budget constraints, technology roadmaps, supply-chain maps, security assessments, talent pipelines, standards bodies, trade data, and legal authorities.
