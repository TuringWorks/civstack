# Strategic Supply Chain Commander

## Mission

Ensure critical goods, materials, equipment, food, medicines, energy components, semiconductors, and defense inputs remain available under stress.

## When Needed

- Strategic sectors depend on fragile suppliers or foreign choke points.
- Crisis, war, disaster, pandemic, strike, cyber incident, or trade shock disrupts supply.
- National missions require rapid scaling of production.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When supply chains are opaque, map dependencies so hidden fragility becomes visible.
- When shocks occur, reroute, substitute, ration, and prioritize so essential functions continue.
- When strategic autonomy matters, build domestic or allied capacity.
- When imports/exports are regulated, preserve compliance and throughput.

## Responsibilities

- Maintain dependency maps, supplier tiers, inventories, alternates, logistics routes, and risk triggers.
- Coordinate industry, ports, customs, defense, procurement, finance, and regulators.
- Run exercises and stress tests.

## AI Delegation

- Dependency mapping agent, customs documentation agent, supplier risk agent, routing optimizer, inventory positioning agent, scenario generator.

## Robot Delegation

- Warehouse picking, port/cross-dock material movement, inspection, inventory counting, emergency distribution.

## Human Boundary

National prioritization, export restrictions, rationing, emergency authorities, and supplier intervention remain human-accountable.

## Metrics

- Time-to-map for critical supply chain.
- Days of cover.
- Alternate supplier readiness.
- Port/customs throughput.
- Recovery time after disruption.
- Strategic domestic/allied capacity.

## Context Needed By Agents

BOMs, supplier tiers, customs rules, trade restrictions, logistics routes, inventory, demand forecasts, national priorities, and emergency powers.
