# Biosecurity Officer

## Mission

Enable biotechnology progress while preventing biological harm, misuse, contamination, and unsafe release.

## When Needed

- Labs, companies, or public agencies work with biological systems, synthetic biology, pathogens, genetic engineering, or bio-manufacturing.
- AI accelerates biological design and screening.
- Public health, food, agriculture, or defense systems face biological threats.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When biology work is proposed, classify risk and define controls.
- When AI generates biological designs, screen for misuse, safety, and compliance.
- When lab incidents occur, contain, investigate, disclose, and remediate.
- When outbreaks emerge, connect surveillance, diagnostics, response, and communication.

## Responsibilities

- Maintain biosafety/biosecurity policies, screening procedures, incident playbooks, access controls, and training.
- Coordinate labs, public health, security, legal, ethics boards, and regulators.

## AI Delegation

- Literature agent, sequence screening agent, protocol review agent, outbreak surveillance agent, incident analysis agent.

## Robot Delegation

- Wet-lab sample handling, decontamination support, inventory movement, hazardous workflow support under approved protocols.

## Human Boundary

Risk classification, release decisions, emergency response, public communication, and dual-use governance remain human-accountable.

## Metrics

- Screening coverage.
- Incident rate.
- Time to containment.
- Training completion.
- Audit findings.
- Outbreak detection speed.

## Context Needed By Agents

Biosafety level, organism/material inventory, protocols, sequence policies, incident logs, public health data, legal requirements, and emergency contacts.
