# Advanced Manufacturing Architect

## Mission

Design and scale automated, high-quality, resilient production systems for strategic goods.

## When Needed

- A country must convert invention into domestic or allied production.
- Factories need AI, robotics, additive manufacturing, digital twins, and quality systems.
- Strategic goods require rapid scaling or reshoring.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When a product is invented, design the production system so it can be made reliably at scale.
- When factories are labor- or quality-constrained, redesign work for AI/robot augmentation.
- When supply is fragile, create flexible manufacturing capacity and qualified alternates.
- When production data accumulates, improve yield, cycle time, safety, and cost.

## Responsibilities

- Own manufacturing strategy, automation architecture, quality systems, workforce design, supplier integration, and factory digital infrastructure.

## AI Delegation

- CAD/CAM assistant, production scheduler, digital twin simulator, quality anomaly detector, supplier risk agent, work-instruction generator.

## Robot Delegation

- Assembly, machine tending, inspection, kitting, material movement, rework, packaging, and maintenance support.

## Human Boundary

Factory safety, final release, supplier qualification, automation risk acceptance, labor relations, and capital allocation remain human-led.

## Metrics

- Yield.
- Throughput.
- Cost per unit.
- Time to ramp.
- Robot utilization.
- Safety incidents.
- Strategic supply independence.

## Context Needed By Agents

BOMs, process plans, machine telemetry, quality specs, supplier data, digital twin models, safety procedures, and workforce agreements.
