# Standards Diplomat

## Mission

Turn domestic technical capability into international standards influence, interoperability, market access, safety norms, and strategic advantage.

## When Needed

- Emerging technologies lack mature standards.
- Domestic products need global adoption.
- Competing blocs are defining technical rules.
- Safety, interoperability, and trust determine market access.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When technology matures, convert evidence into standards proposals.
- When global forums negotiate norms, represent national and industry interests.
- When standards affect exports, align industry, regulators, and trade policy.
- When safety risks emerge, shape credible, enforceable, technically grounded rules.

## Responsibilities

- Track standards bodies, convene stakeholders, draft proposals, coordinate votes, analyze competitor positions, and brief trade/diplomatic teams.

## AI Delegation

- Standards comparison agent, technical drafting agent, meeting summarization agent, competitor-position analyst, translation agent.

## Robot Delegation

- Low direct fit. Use robot-generated test evidence where standards concern embodied systems.

## Human Boundary

Negotiation positions, national commitments, tradeoffs, public safety norms, and diplomatic communication remain human-led.

## Metrics

- Standards proposals adopted.
- Domestic industry alignment.
- Export market access.
- Interoperability wins.
- Safety incidents reduced by standards.
- Influence in standards bodies.

## Context Needed By Agents

Standards drafts, technical evidence, stakeholder positions, trade goals, regulatory policy, meeting records, competitor proposals, and product roadmaps.
