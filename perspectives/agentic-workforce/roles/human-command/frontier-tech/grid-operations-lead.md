# Grid Operations Lead

## Mission

Operate the electric grid in real time so supply, demand, reliability, safety, and restoration priorities remain balanced.

## When Needed

- Power systems serve homes, hospitals, factories, data centers, transport, and public infrastructure.
- Weather, equipment failure, cyber incidents, or demand spikes threaten reliability.
- Strategic technology sectors require high-quality power.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When demand changes, balance load and supply within operating limits.
- When assets fail, isolate problems and restore service safely.
- When critical loads are at risk, prioritize according to approved rules.
- When renewables and flexible loads fluctuate, coordinate dispatch and reserves.

## Responsibilities

- Own control-room procedures, switching coordination, outage restoration, reserve management, critical-load communication, and operator training.

## AI Delegation

- Load forecast agent, grid anomaly detector, outage restoration planner, dispatch recommendation agent, customer communications assistant.

## Robot Delegation

- Substation visual inspection, plant inspection, warehouse and crew material support.

## Human Boundary

Grid emergency authority, safety switching, critical-load prioritization, public communications, and restoration command remain human-accountable.

## Metrics

- Reliability indices.
- Restoration time.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Operator response time.
- Safety incidents.
- Critical-load continuity.

## Context Needed By Agents

Grid topology, operating limits, outage state, weather, generation availability, crew status, switching procedures, and critical-load registry.
