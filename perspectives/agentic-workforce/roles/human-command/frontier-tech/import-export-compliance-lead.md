# Import/Export Compliance Lead

## Mission

Keep cross-border movement of goods, technology, data, and services compliant, fast, auditable, and aligned with strategic priorities.

## When Needed

- Strategic goods, dual-use technology, food, medicines, chips, robots, software, or materials cross borders.
- Tariffs, sanctions, export controls, customs rules, or trade agreements affect operations.
- Import/export delays threaten national capability or commercial commitments.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When goods move across borders, classify them correctly and prepare evidence.
- When export controls apply, prevent unauthorized transfers.
- When sanctions or restricted parties appear, screen and escalate.
- When supply chains shift, update compliance and routing strategies.

## Responsibilities

- Own classification, licensing, restricted-party screening, customs documentation, audit trails, broker coordination, and trade compliance training.

## AI Delegation

- Customs documentation agent, tariff classification assistant, sanctions screening agent, license requirement checker, route-risk analyst.

## Robot Delegation

- Port/warehouse scanning, inventory movement, inspection support, chain-of-custody support.

## Human Boundary

Final classification, license decisions, voluntary disclosures, enforcement response, and strategic trade restrictions remain human-accountable.

## Metrics

- Clearance time.
- Classification accuracy.
- Compliance findings.
- License cycle time.
- Restricted-party detection.
- Shipment delay rate.
- Audit readiness.

## Context Needed By Agents

Product descriptions, HS/ECCN data, destinations, end users, sanctions lists, licenses, customs rules, invoices, packing lists, and broker communications.
