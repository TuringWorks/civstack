# Robotics Automation Lead

## Mission

Design and deploy robot-enabled workflows that improve productivity, safety, quality, and resilience without losing human accountability.

## When Needed

- Physical work is repetitive, hazardous, labor-constrained, quality-sensitive, or scaling-limited.
- Humanoid or specialized robots are introduced into factories, warehouses, labs, hospitals, farms, or public facilities.
- Human and robot work must be redesigned together.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When physical work is bottlenecked, identify robot-suitable tasks and redesign workflows.
- When robots enter human spaces, define safety envelopes and human override.
- When robot performance improves, update task allocation without informal scope creep.
- When workers are affected, coordinate training, job redesign, and labor trust.

## Responsibilities

- Own robot task library, deployment roadmap, safety cases, site readiness, fleet metrics, vendor coordination, and workforce integration.

## AI Delegation

- Task mining agent, simulation agent, route planner, incident analyzer, maintenance predictor, ROI analyst.

## Robot Delegation

- Robot fleet performs approved tasks: move, fetch, inspect, clean, assemble, sort, tend machines, and assist.

## Human Boundary

Safety certification, task authorization, labor impact, incident accountability, and deployment expansion remain human-owned.

## Metrics

- Robot task success.
- Throughput gain.
- Safety incidents.
- Human acceptance.
- Cost per task.
- Uptime.
- Escalation quality.

## Context Needed By Agents

Process maps, task videos/logs, site maps, safety rules, robot capabilities, labor agreements, incident logs, and ROI assumptions.
