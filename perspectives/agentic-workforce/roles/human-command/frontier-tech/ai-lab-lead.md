# AI Lab Lead

## Mission

Set the research and product direction for AI model capability while ensuring the lab converts compute, data, talent, and evaluation into useful, safe systems.

## When Needed

- An institution builds foundation models, specialized models, or AI-native products.
- Compute and data budgets are large enough to require portfolio decisions.
- Model capability creates strategic, commercial, or safety consequences.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When AI capability is uncertain, define research bets so teams explore the highest-value frontier.
- When compute is scarce, allocate experiments so learning per dollar is maximized.
- When models become products, coordinate research, platform, safety, product, and go-to-market.
- When risks emerge, slow or redirect work before harm scales.

## Responsibilities

- Set model roadmap, research agenda, staffing, compute allocation, collaboration model, release philosophy, and evaluation gates.
- Maintain a portfolio across scaling, data, architecture, tools, agents, multimodality, efficiency, safety, and productization.

## AI Delegation

- Literature agent, experiment planner, benchmark analyst, training-run summarizer, model comparison agent, roadmap synthesis agent.

## Robot Delegation

- Low direct fit except for embodied AI labs, data center logistics, and hardware testing.

## Human Boundary

Research priorities, compute allocation, release decisions, safety tradeoffs, hiring, partner strategy, and public claims remain human-led.

## Metrics

- Capability improvements.
- Learning per compute dollar.
- Evaluation pass rate.
- Product adoption.
- Safety incident rate.
- Talent retention.
- Release cycle quality.

## Context Needed By Agents

Research roadmap, experiment logs, model cards, eval reports, compute budget, data plan, incident history, product feedback, and competitive landscape.
