# Accountability Boundaries

These actions require human or institutional accountability even when AI or robots perform supporting work.

- Coercive power: arrest, detention, search, seizure, use of force, military escalation.
- Rights-impacting decisions: benefit denial, asylum, sentencing, eviction, credit denial appeals, public-service exclusion.
- Intimate care: childcare, therapy, end-of-life care, disability support, spiritual care.
- Democratic legitimacy: lawmaking, public budgeting, constitutional interpretation, emergency powers.
- High-consequence safety: surgery, grid emergency control, aviation control, nuclear operations, hazardous-process shutdown.
- Ethical tradeoffs: land use, environmental justice, triage, labor standards, relocation.
- Regulated professional signoff: legal, medical, engineering, financial, audit, safety certification.
- AI governance: deployment approval, model-risk acceptance, redress, incident disclosure.

For every delegated task, define:

- Who owns the outcome.
- What the AI/robot may decide without review.
- What must be escalated.
- What evidence must be logged.
- How affected people can appeal or seek redress.
