# Food, Agriculture, Fisheries, and Nutrition Workforce

## Mission

Produce, inspect, distribute, and stabilize safe food and nutrition.

## Core Jobs

- Grow, raise, catch, process, transport, and sell food.
- Manage pests, drought, disease, soil, water, and climate stress.
- Preserve food safety, freshness, traceability, and labeling.
- Stabilize supply through storage, imports, exports, and reserves.
- Target nutrition support where food insecurity exists.

## Human Role Families

- Farmer, ranch manager, farmworker, fishery manager, aquaculture technician.
- Agronomist, soil scientist, crop advisor, irrigation specialist.
- Food scientist, quality assurance manager, food safety inspector.
- Veterinarian, animal health technician, livestock nutritionist.
- Grain merchandiser, cold-chain logistics planner, food distribution manager.
- Dietitian, school nutrition director, food assistance program manager.
- Agricultural trade analyst, commodity risk manager, import/export compliance specialist.

## AI Personnel

- Crop planning agent: optimize crop mix, rotation, irrigation, and input timing.
- Pest and disease detection agent: analyze imagery, sensor data, and reports.
- Yield forecast agent: combine weather, soil, satellite, and field data.
- Food safety compliance agent: map process controls to inspection evidence.
- Traceability analyst: track lots from origin to consumer.
- Commodity market agent: monitor price, supply, weather, freight, and trade policy.
- Nutrition optimizer: design menus and assistance packages.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Greenhouse worker, sorting and packing assistant.
- Harvest support where crop geometry is suitable.
- Cold-chain warehouse picker and sanitation assistant.
- Livestock barn inspection and feed support under supervision.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep animal welfare, pesticide decisions, land stewardship, labor conditions, import/export restrictions, food safety certification, and public nutrition policy human-accountable.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load crop calendars, soil/water data, food safety plans, import/export rules, cold-chain requirements, commodity contracts, animal welfare standards, and inspection history.
