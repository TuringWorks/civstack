# Labor, Workforce Systems, and Organizational Life Workforce

## Mission

Match people to work, protect workers, build organizations, and maintain productive cultures.

## Core Jobs

- Define roles, recruit, assess, hire, onboard, train, manage, pay, and retain.
- Enforce labor standards and remedy harm.
- Redesign jobs and reskill people as technology changes work.
- Set goals, communicate, resolve conflict, and maintain culture.

## Human Role Families

- Recruiter, talent acquisition partner, sourcer, HR business partner.
- Compensation analyst, benefits administrator, payroll specialist.
- Learning and development manager, organizational development consultant.
- Labor relations specialist, employment lawyer, workplace investigator.
- Chief people officer, operations chief, change manager.
- AI workforce transformation lead, automation program manager.

## AI Personnel

- Job description agent: draft role definitions, responsibilities, and rubrics.
- Candidate matching assistant: compare evidence to job criteria with bias controls.
- Interview scheduling agent: coordinate logistics and communications.
- Skills inference agent: map work history to capability models.
- Training recommender: suggest reskilling pathways.
- Workforce planning simulator: model demand, capacity, automation, and hiring.
- Employee sentiment analyst: summarize themes for human review.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Workplace facilities support.
- Physical task augmentation and training simulation companion.
- Safety observation support in industrial environments.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep hiring, firing, discipline, compensation, pay equity, union negotiation, harassment investigations, accommodation, and culture leadership human-accountable.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load job architecture, legal requirements, labor agreements, compensation bands, performance criteria, accommodation policy, training catalog, and automation impact assessments.
