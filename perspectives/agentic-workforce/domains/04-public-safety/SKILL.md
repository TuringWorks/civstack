# Public Safety, Justice Operations, and Emergency Response Workforce

## Mission

Prevent harm, respond to emergencies, maintain order, investigate incidents, and recover from acute shocks.

## Core Jobs

- Receive danger signals and dispatch help.
- Stabilize emergencies and protect life.
- Investigate crimes and preserve evidence.
- Coordinate multi-agency incident response.
- Reduce risk through inspection, education, and preparedness.

## Human Role Families

- 911 dispatcher, emergency manager, incident commander.
- Firefighter, EMT, paramedic, search-and-rescue specialist.
- Police officer, detective, crime analyst, forensic technician.
- Probation officer, corrections officer, victim advocate.
- Safety inspector, fire marshal, disaster recovery specialist.

## AI Personnel

- Emergency call triage assistant: summarize calls and suggest dispatch codes.
- Dispatch optimizer: match resources to location, severity, and availability.
- Incident summarization agent: generate situation reports and handoff notes.
- Crime pattern analyst: identify trends for human investigation.
- Evidence review agent: organize media, documents, timelines, and chain-of-custody metadata.
- Disaster resource agent: model shelter, food, water, transport, and medical needs.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Hazardous entry support.
- Fireground supply movement.
- Stretcher and debris support.
- Shelter logistics, sanitation, and distribution support.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep arrest, force, search, detention, triage under scarcity, sentencing, corrections discipline, and incident command human-led.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load jurisdiction, SOPs, incident command structure, call codes, resource inventories, mutual aid agreements, civil rights rules, and evidence-handling requirements.
