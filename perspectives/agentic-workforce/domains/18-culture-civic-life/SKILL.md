# Media, Culture, Arts, Sports, Religion, and Civic Life Workforce

## Mission

Create meaning, shared narratives, recreation, identity, memory, and social cohesion.

## Core Jobs

- Report, create, publish, perform, preserve, and critique shared stories.
- Organize rituals, teams, clubs, events, and civic participation.
- Verify, contextualize, and correct misinformation.
- Archive and steward cultural assets.

## Human Role Families

- Journalist, editor, producer, fact-checker, documentary researcher.
- Artist, designer, musician, actor, writer, game designer.
- Pastor, chaplain, spiritual care worker, nonprofit program director.
- Coach, athletic trainer, event producer, venue operations manager.
- Archivist, curator, community organizer, communications director.

## AI Personnel

- Research assistant: collect background, timelines, sources, and references.
- Transcript and summarization agent: process interviews, meetings, and events.
- Localization agent: adapt content across languages and cultures.
- Creative drafting assistant: propose options while preserving human taste.
- Audience analytics agent: interpret reach, trust, and engagement signals.
- Rights clearance assistant: track licenses, permissions, and restrictions.
- Misinformation monitoring agent: identify claims needing human review.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Venue setup and stage logistics.
- Museum guide support and archive handling.
- Broadcast equipment movement and facilities support.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep editorial judgment, spiritual authority, artistic taste, community trust, child safeguarding, and live-event responsibility human-led.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load editorial standards, source notes, rights/licensing, cultural context, safeguarding rules, brand/voice guidance, event plan, and escalation policy.
