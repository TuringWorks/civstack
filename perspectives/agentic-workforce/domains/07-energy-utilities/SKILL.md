# Energy, Utilities, and Grid Operations Workforce

## Mission

Produce, store, transmit, distribute, and balance energy safely, affordably, and resiliently.

## Core Jobs

- Balance supply and demand second by second.
- Maintain generation, storage, transmission, and distribution assets.
- Plan resilient fuel, power, and capacity supply.
- Integrate renewables, storage, demand response, nuclear, hydro, geothermal, and efficiency.
- Restore outages safely and communicate clearly.

## Human Role Families

- Grid operator, power systems engineer, utility dispatcher.
- Electrician, lineworker, substation technician, relay technician.
- Renewable energy engineer, solar installer, wind turbine technician.
- Nuclear operator, plant engineer, safety analyst.
- Energy trader, load forecaster, demand response manager.
- Utility customer operations manager, field service technician.

## AI Personnel

- Load forecasting agent: predict demand by weather, calendar, and economic activity.
- Grid anomaly detector: surface overloads, frequency events, and asset failures.
- Outage restoration planner: recommend switching, crew dispatch, and communications.
- Maintenance prediction agent: rank inspection and repair work.
- Energy market analyst: monitor price, congestion, fuel, and regulatory signals.
- Customer outage communications agent: produce status updates and restoration estimates.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Plant inspection and meter/gauge rounds.
- Solar farm and wind-site maintenance support.
- Substation visual inspection under strict safety envelope.
- Warehouse and crew material support.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep grid emergency authority, nuclear operations, energized switching, safety clearances, market conduct, and infrastructure siting human-accountable.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load grid topology, operating limits, safety switching procedures, outage maps, asset telemetry, weather, market rules, and emergency restoration plans.
