# Healthcare, Public Health, and Biomedical Systems Workforce

## Mission

Prevent disease, diagnose and treat illness, rehabilitate people, and support health across populations.

## Core Jobs

- Diagnose, treat, monitor, comfort, and follow up.
- Surveil, trace, vaccinate, communicate, and coordinate during outbreaks.
- Research, test, approve, produce, prescribe, and monitor medicines/devices.
- Coordinate records, referrals, coverage, and home support.
- Triage ethically and transparently when resources are scarce.

## Human Role Families

- Physician, nurse practitioner, physician assistant, nurse, pharmacist.
- Medical assistant, phlebotomist, radiologic technologist, lab technician.
- Therapist, psychologist, social worker, care coordinator.
- Epidemiologist, public health nurse, infection preventionist.
- Hospital administrator, revenue cycle analyst, health informatics specialist.
- Clinical researcher, regulatory affairs specialist, biomedical engineer.
- Home health aide, eldercare worker, rehabilitation aide.

## AI Personnel

- Clinical documentation agent: draft notes, summaries, and coding support.
- Prior authorization agent: prepare evidence packets and status tracking.
- Diagnostic support agent: surface differential considerations and evidence.
- Imaging triage assistant: prioritize studies for human review.
- Public health surveillance agent: detect outbreak signals.
- Clinical trial matching agent: match patients, criteria, sites, and studies.
- Care gap analyst: identify missed screenings, medications, and follow-ups.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Supply delivery, room turnover, and medication transport.
- Lifting support and sample movement.
- Elder support: fetch, remind, monitor, and assist with mobility.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep diagnosis, prescribing, surgery, consent, triage, end-of-life decisions, patient relationship accountability, and public health orders human-led.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load clinical guidelines, patient context, consent status, scope-of-practice rules, medication data, lab/imaging results, privacy rules, and escalation protocols.
