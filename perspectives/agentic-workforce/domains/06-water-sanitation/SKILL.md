# Water, Sanitation, and Public Hygiene Workforce

## Mission

Provide safe water, remove waste, control flooding, and prevent waterborne disease.

## Core Jobs

- Collect, treat, distribute, meter, and maintain water.
- Collect, treat, discharge, reuse, or recover wastewater resources.
- Manage drainage and flood protection.
- Test, notify, isolate, and remediate contamination.
- Maintain public hygiene infrastructure.

## Human Role Families

- Water treatment operator, wastewater operator, utility technician.
- Civil engineer, environmental engineer, hydrologist, water resource planner.
- Plumber, pipefitter, leak detection technician, meter technician.
- Public health inspector, laboratory technician, environmental compliance specialist.
- Floodplain manager, stormwater program manager.

## AI Personnel

- Water quality monitoring agent: detect abnormal test patterns and recommend sampling.
- Leak prediction agent: prioritize repairs using pressure, flow, and acoustic data.
- Pump optimization agent: reduce energy use while maintaining service.
- Permit compliance reviewer: compare discharge evidence to permit limits.
- Flood forecast analyst: combine weather, terrain, drainage, and asset data.
- Asset maintenance planner: rank pipe, pump, valve, and plant work.

## Humanoid Robot Roles

- Plant rounds and sample transport.
- Valve-turning and meter-reading support.
- Pipe repair assistant.
- Emergency logistics and flood-control support.

## Accountability Boundary

Keep public health notices, shutoffs, emergency allocation, environmental discharge approvals, and capital investment priorities human-led.

## Agent Context Requirements

Load water quality standards, permits, utility maps, asset records, lab procedures, emergency notices, flood models, and public health escalation rules.
