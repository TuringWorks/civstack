# Autonomous Agriculture Operating Model

Purpose: coordinate autonomous tractors, sprayers, harvesters, field carts, agricultural drones, irrigation robots, and crop-perception systems.

## Mission

Produce food and agricultural outputs with higher precision, labor leverage, resilience, and environmental stewardship.

## Human Command Roles

- Farm autonomy manager.
- Harvest operations lead.
- Agronomy operations lead.
- Crop quality manager.
- Equipment fleet manager.
- Food safety/traceability lead.
- Pesticide/application compliance lead.

## AI Personnel

- Field mission planner.
- Crop/weed detection agent.
- Precision application agent.
- Harvest timing agent.
- Yield mapping agent.
- Weather window agent.
- Soil compaction analyst.
- Equipment health agent.

## Autonomous Machines

- Autonomous tractor.
- Autonomous planter/seeder.
- Autonomous sprayer.
- Autonomous harvester.
- Robotic picker.
- Autonomous field cart/bin shuttle.
- Agricultural scouting/spray drone.
- Irrigation inspection robot.

## Operating Loop

1. Define crop plan, field boundaries, prescriptions, and season goals.
2. Map hazards, exclusion zones, soil constraints, and sensitive areas.
3. Schedule autonomous field passes around weather, crop stage, and input labels.
4. Dispatch machines and monitor operations.
5. Verify application, planting, cultivation, scouting, or harvest completion.
6. Link yield/quality data to field zones and machine actions.
7. Update agronomic plan and machine routes.

## Required Artifacts

- Field map.
- Crop plan.
- Input prescription.
- Equipment task envelope.
- Worker/animal exclusion plan.
- Food safety and traceability plan.
- Weather limits.
- Maintenance plan.

## Metrics

- Yield.
- Input use efficiency.
- Crop damage.
- Soil compaction.
- Harvest loss.
- Food safety incidents.
- Machine uptime.
- Labor hours shifted to supervision/analysis.
