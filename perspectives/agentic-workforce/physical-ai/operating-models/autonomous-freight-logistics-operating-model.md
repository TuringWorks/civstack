# Autonomous Freight And Logistics Operating Model

Purpose: coordinate autonomous trucks, yard tractors, delivery vehicles, warehouse AMRs, autonomous forklifts, drones, and port equipment.

## Mission

Move goods through corridors, hubs, ports, warehouses, and last-mile networks with higher reliability, safety, and throughput.

## Human Command Roles

- Freight autonomy lead.
- Fleet safety operator.
- Warehouse automation lead.
- Port autonomy operations lead.
- Import/export compliance lead.
- Remote operations manager.
- Maintenance and charging/fueling lead.

## AI Personnel

- Routing optimizer.
- Customs documentation agent.
- Fleet dispatch agent.
- Warehouse orchestration agent.
- Inventory accuracy agent.
- Congestion/risk agent.
- Maintenance prediction agent.
- Incident response copilot.

## Autonomous Machines

- Autonomous truck.
- Autonomous yard tractor.
- Autonomous delivery vehicle.
- Autonomous forklift.
- Warehouse AMR.
- Inventory scanning robot.
- Delivery drone.
- Port material handler.

## Operating Loop

1. Receive orders, cargo, constraints, customs/trade rules, and delivery promises.
2. Plan route, hub, warehouse, and machine assignments.
3. Verify vehicle/machine readiness and legal operating domain.
4. Dispatch autonomous machines and monitor exceptions.
5. Coordinate handoffs between road, port, warehouse, drone, and last-mile systems.
6. Verify delivery, inventory, and documentation.
7. Review incidents, damage, delays, and compliance issues.

## Required Artifacts

- Cargo manifest.
- Route/geofence plan.
- Warehouse/facility map.
- Customs/trade document set.
- Fleet maintenance plan.
- Incident playbook.
- Worker safety plan.

## Metrics

- On-time delivery.
- Cost per shipment.
- Warehouse throughput.
- Customs clearance time.
- Damage rate.
- Safety incidents.
- Fleet utilization.
- Emissions/energy per shipment.
