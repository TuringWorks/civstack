# Physical AI Fleet Operating Model

Purpose: coordinate fleets of non-humanoid autonomous machines across roads, fields, warehouses, mines, ports, farms, airspace, rail corridors, marine environments, and industrial sites.

## Mission

Deploy autonomous machines safely, productively, and accountably in bounded operating domains.

## Human Command Roles

- Physical AI fleet director.
- Fleet safety operator.
- Remote operations manager.
- Site autonomy superintendent.
- Machine maintenance lead.
- Safety case engineer.
- Incident investigator.
- Regulatory/compliance lead.

## AI Personnel

- Mission planning agent.
- Route/geofence risk analyst.
- Fleet dispatch agent.
- Perception failure analyst.
- Maintenance prediction agent.
- Incident response copilot.
- Simulation scenario generator.
- Safety zone monitor.

## Autonomous Machines

- Road vehicles, trucks, shuttles, yard tractors.
- Farm tractors, sprayers, harvesters, field carts.
- Drones and aerial robots.
- Warehouse AMRs and autonomous forklifts.
- Loaders, haul trucks, excavators, dozers, graders.
- Rail, marine, and underwater systems.
- Fixed industrial robotic cells.

## Operating Loop

1. Define mission, site/route, operating design domain, and human owner.
2. Build maps, geofences, routes, exclusion zones, and task plans.
3. Verify machine readiness, sensors, tools, payloads, communications, and maintenance state.
4. Assign mission through fleet dispatch.
5. Monitor mission with AI and human remote operations.
6. Intervene, pause, or safe-stop when stop conditions occur.
7. Verify task completion and physical evidence.
8. Review logs, incidents, near misses, and performance.
9. Update maps, models, SOPs, training, and safety case.

## Required Artifacts

- Machine role card.
- Operating design domain.
- Site/route map.
- Geofence/exclusion zone plan.
- Mission plan.
- Remote operations plan.
- Maintenance plan.
- Incident playbook.
- Safety case.
- Cybersecurity plan.
- Training record.

## Metrics

- Task completion.
- Safety incidents and near misses.
- Human interventions per mission.
- Autonomy availability.
- Cost per task.
- Machine utilization.
- Maintenance downtime.
- Damage rate.
- Public/worker acceptance.
