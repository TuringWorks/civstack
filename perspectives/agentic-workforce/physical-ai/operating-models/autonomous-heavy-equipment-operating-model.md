# Autonomous Heavy Equipment Operating Model

Purpose: coordinate autonomous loaders, mine loaders, haul trucks, excavators, dozers, graders, compactors, and port/bulk-material handlers.

## Mission

Move earth, ore, aggregate, containers, debris, and bulk materials safely and productively in controlled sites.

## Human Command Roles

- Heavy equipment autonomy lead.
- Site autonomy superintendent.
- Mine autonomy manager.
- Equipment dispatcher.
- Remote operator.
- Safety supervisor.
- Maintenance lead.
- Geotechnical/survey lead.

## AI Personnel

- Site mission planner.
- Terrain and stability analyst.
- Fleet dispatch agent.
- Payload optimization agent.
- Maintenance prediction agent.
- Safety zone monitor.
- Incident response copilot.

## Autonomous Machines

- Autonomous loader.
- Autonomous mine loader.
- Autonomous haul truck.
- Autonomous excavator.
- Autonomous dozer.
- Autonomous grader.
- Autonomous compactor.
- Autonomous port/bulk-material handler.

## Operating Loop

1. Load site plan, survey model, exclusion zones, and task schedule.
2. Assign machines to routes, stockpiles, dig zones, haul cycles, and dump points.
3. Verify machine health, payload constraints, ground conditions, and weather.
4. Execute material movement or earthmoving under remote monitoring.
5. Detect people, vehicles, instability, tool faults, and geofence violations.
6. Verify volume, grade, compaction, or payload results.
7. Review safety, productivity, and maintenance data.

## Required Artifacts

- Site map and survey model.
- Traffic management plan.
- Exclusion zone plan.
- Geotechnical risk register.
- Machine task envelope.
- Maintenance plan.
- Emergency response plan.

## Metrics

- Tons/cubic yards moved.
- Cycle time.
- Fuel/energy per unit moved.
- Grade/volume accuracy.
- Safety incidents.
- Machine downtime.
- Payload efficiency.
- Worker exposure reduction.
