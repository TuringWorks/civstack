# Physical AI Fleet Director

## Mission

Own strategy, safety, economics, and operations for fleets of non-humanoid autonomous machines.

## When Needed

- Autonomous machines operate across sites, roads, fields, warehouses, airspace, ports, or industrial facilities.
- Multiple machine types require common safety, maintenance, data, and remote-operations standards.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- When autonomy is proposed, choose tasks where machine form, operating domain, and safety case make sense.
- When machines operate, ensure every mission has a human owner, approved ODD, logs, and intervention path.
- When incidents happen, investigate, remediate, and update the safety case.
- When performance data accumulates, improve routing, task design, maintenance, and ROI.

## AI Delegation

- Fleet dispatch agent, mission planning agent, maintenance prediction agent, incident response copilot, safety zone monitor, route risk analyst.

## Machine Delegation

- Autonomous vehicles, tractors, harvesters, drones, AMRs, forklifts, loaders, haul trucks, rail/marine systems, and fixed robotic cells.

## Human Boundary

Deployment approval, safety case acceptance, incident accountability, public/worker impact, regulatory commitments, and operating-domain expansion remain human-owned.

## Metrics

- Fleet utilization.
- Task completion.
- Safety incidents.
- Human interventions.
- Machine downtime.
- Cost per task.
- Damage rate.
- Regulatory findings.

## Context Needed By Agents

Fleet inventory, ODD definitions, site maps, routes, machine capabilities, safety policies, incident logs, maintenance records, and regulatory constraints.
