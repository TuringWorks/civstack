# Autonomous Farm Equipment Workforce

## Mission

Increase agricultural productivity, precision, resilience, and labor leverage while protecting workers, animals, crops, soil, water, and ecosystems.

## Best Use Cases

- Autonomous tillage, planting, seeding, cultivation, spraying, mowing, and hauling.
- Precision input application by zone, crop, soil, and weather.
- Orchard and vineyard operations.
- Field scouting and sensor-guided crop management.
- Night or low-labor field operations under approved conditions.
- Repetitive field passes where boundaries and hazards are mapped.

## Human Role Families

- Farm autonomy manager, agronomy operations lead, equipment fleet manager.
- Precision agriculture specialist, crop advisor, irrigation specialist.
- Farm safety officer, maintenance technician, field operations supervisor.
- Pesticide/application compliance lead, soil health steward, data manager.

## AI Personnel

- Field mission planner: convert agronomic plan into routes, implements, timing, and constraints.
- Crop/weed detection agent: identify crop state, weeds, disease, pests, and gaps.
- Precision application agent: recommend variable-rate inputs within human-approved prescription.
- Weather window agent: identify safe and effective operating windows.
- Soil compaction analyst: optimize traffic patterns to protect soil.
- Equipment health agent: predict failures and maintenance needs.

## Autonomous Machine Roles

- Autonomous tractor: pull implements and haul materials.
- Autonomous planter/seeder: place seed precisely.
- Autonomous sprayer: apply pesticides, fertilizer, or biological inputs.
- Autonomous cultivator/weed machine: remove weeds mechanically or precisely.
- Autonomous irrigation robot: inspect, adjust, and maintain irrigation systems.

## Operating Design Domain

Define field boundaries, crop type, terrain, slope, soil conditions, people/animal exclusion zones, weather limits, implement/tool limits, input application rules, and emergency stops.

## Safety And Accountability

Keep pesticide/input approval, animal safety, worker exclusion zones, crop stewardship, soil/water protection, and food safety human-accountable.

## Required Context For Agents

Field maps, crop plan, soil data, prescriptions, input labels, weather, equipment capability, implement specs, safety rules, maintenance logs, and agronomic thresholds.
