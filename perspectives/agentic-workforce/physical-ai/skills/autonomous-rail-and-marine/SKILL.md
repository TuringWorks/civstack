# Autonomous Rail And Marine Workforce

## Mission

Move people, goods, and inspection payloads through rail and water networks with high reliability, safety, and coordination.

## Best Use Cases

- Automated metro and people mover systems.
- Rail yard switching and inspection.
- Autonomous freight train support in controlled corridors.
- Autonomous port vessels and tug-assist support.
- Survey vessels for mapping and environmental monitoring.
- Underwater robots for cables, pipelines, dams, bridges, and ports.

## Human Role Families

- Rail operations authority, marine autonomy lead, port operations lead.
- Dispatch/control center operator, remote vessel operator, yard automation lead.
- Safety case engineer, maintenance lead, incident commander.
- Hydrographic survey lead, subsea operations lead, regulatory compliance lead.

## AI Personnel

- Rail dispatch optimization agent.
- Marine route and collision-risk agent.
- Yard sequencing agent.
- Vessel/track inspection analysis agent.
- Weather/tide/current risk agent.
- Maintenance prediction agent.
- Incident response copilot.

## Autonomous Machine Roles

- Automated metro/train.
- Autonomous rail inspection vehicle.
- Autonomous yard locomotive.
- Autonomous survey vessel.
- Autonomous port vessel.
- Underwater inspection robot.

## Operating Design Domain

Define track/waterway corridor, signaling/control integration, speed, traffic separation, weather/water conditions, remote operations, emergency stops, passenger/cargo policy, and regulatory requirements.

## Safety And Accountability

Keep passenger safety, collision avoidance policy, route authorization, port/rail coordination, incident disclosure, and regulatory approval human-accountable.

## Required Context For Agents

Track/water maps, schedules, signaling data, vessel/vehicle capability, weather/tide/current, maintenance logs, cargo/passenger rules, incident playbooks, and control authority.
