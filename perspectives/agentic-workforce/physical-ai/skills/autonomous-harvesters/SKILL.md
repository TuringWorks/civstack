# Autonomous Harvesters Workforce

## Mission

Harvest crops at the right time, quality, yield, and cost while protecting workers, soil, equipment, and downstream food systems.

## Best Use Cases

- Grain and row-crop combining.
- Orchard/vineyard harvesting.
- Controlled-environment produce picking.
- Vegetable harvesting with machine-vision quality selection.
- Harvest logistics: field carts, bin movement, and transport staging.
- Crop maturity scouting and harvest sequencing.

## Human Role Families

- Harvest operations lead, farm autonomy manager, crop quality manager.
- Machine maintenance lead, food safety/traceability lead, field logistics coordinator.
- Agronomist, commodity merchandiser, cold-chain planner.

## AI Personnel

- Harvest timing agent: recommend harvest windows from maturity, weather, labor, market, and storage.
- Crop quality perception agent: detect ripeness, defects, moisture, size, and contamination.
- Yield mapping agent: generate spatial yield and loss maps.
- Harvest logistics optimizer: coordinate machines, carts, bins, trucks, storage, and cold chain.
- Machine performance analyst: monitor loss, throughput, clogging, fuel/energy, and wear.

## Autonomous Machine Roles

- Autonomous combine harvester.
- Autonomous fruit/nut harvester.
- Autonomous vegetable harvester.
- Robotic picker.
- Autonomous field cart or bin shuttle.

## Operating Design Domain

Define crop type, harvest maturity criteria, field/orchard boundaries, machine head/tool, worker exclusion, soil conditions, weather limits, storage/cold-chain link, and food safety controls.

## Safety And Accountability

Keep harvest timing strategy, food safety, crop quality acceptance, worker safety, animal/bystander safety, and market commitments human-accountable.

## Required Context For Agents

Crop maturity data, weather, field maps, machine telemetry, quality specs, storage capacity, truck schedules, food safety plan, traceability rules, and market contracts.
