# Warehouse AMRs And Autonomous Forklifts Workforce

## Mission

Move, store, pick, sort, replenish, and stage goods safely and efficiently inside logistics and industrial facilities.

## Best Use Cases

- Goods-to-person picking.
- Pallet transport and putaway.
- Autonomous forklift movement.
- Sortation and cross-docking.
- Hospital supply movement.
- Retail backroom replenishment.
- Port/warehouse yard staging.

## Human Role Families

- Warehouse automation lead, material handling safety lead, fulfillment operations manager.
- Fleet operator, maintenance technician, slotting analyst, inventory control lead.
- Safety trainer, labor/workforce transition lead, systems integration lead.

## AI Personnel

- Warehouse orchestration agent: assign AMRs, forklifts, pickers, docks, and waves.
- Slotting optimization agent: place inventory based on demand and constraints.
- Inventory accuracy agent: reconcile scans, counts, orders, and exceptions.
- Congestion/risk agent: detect traffic conflicts and unsafe flows.
- Maintenance prediction agent: forecast batteries, wheels, forks, sensors, and drive issues.

## Autonomous Machine Roles

- AMR tote/cart mover.
- Autonomous forklift.
- Autonomous pallet jack.
- Sortation robot.
- Inventory scanning robot.
- Hospital/material delivery robot.

## Operating Design Domain

Define facility map, speed zones, pedestrian/forklift separation, payloads, rack geometry, dock rules, charging zones, floor conditions, emergency exits, and human interaction rules.

## Safety And Accountability

Keep worker safety, facility layout approval, payload limits, regulated goods handling, incident response, and labor transition human-accountable.

## Required Context For Agents

WMS data, orders, inventory, facility map, robot capabilities, safety rules, labor schedule, dock schedule, maintenance logs, and incident history.
