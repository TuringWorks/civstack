# Procurement plan

## Buying policy

Buy by phase, not by imagined final quantity. Prefer locally stocked plywood,
standard bearings, shaft sizes, fasteners and motors with published curves. Record
supplier, manufacturer part number, lot, unit price and measured mass on receipt.
Planning costs are allowances, not current quotations.

Wood replaces metal where it is structurally and thermally credible. Do not replace
bearing races, shafts, motor internals, electrical contacts, certified fall-arrest
hardware or the E-stop/contactors with wood.

## Phase A — authorize now

- One sheet each of representative 6 mm and 12 mm birch plywood.
- J1 BOM only, including spare tendon and termination materials.
- Fit/kerf coupon stock and sacrificial fasteners.
- Bench power supply or current-limited supply, controller and encoder.
- Guarding, eye protection, fire extinguisher and accessible E-stop.

Acceptance: material thickness and warp recorded; electronics powered without
actuator connection; every received item tied to a BOM row.

## Phase B — after J1 gates

- One class-H actuator/transmission set.
- Laminated hip/knee link stock and proof-load fixture materials.
- Final candidate bearings, shafts and inserts.
- Load cell, temperature sensing and independent angle reference.

## Phase C — after structural proof

- Lower-body actuator sets, bus nodes and harness materials.
- 48 V development supply; battery only after measured power traces exist.
- Overhead gantry and certified fall-arrest equipment.

## Phase D — after tethered mobility

- Upper-body axes, gripper parts, perception computer and sensors.
- Cosmetic laminae only after service clearances are frozen.

## Substitution control

A substitute is drop-in only when envelope, mounting, voltage, current, torque-speed
curve, encoder behavior, thermal rating and safety certification are equal or better.
Otherwise open a design change and repeat affected verification.
