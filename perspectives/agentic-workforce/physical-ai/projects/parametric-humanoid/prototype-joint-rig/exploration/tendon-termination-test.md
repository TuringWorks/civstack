# Tendon Termination Experiment

The termination is likely to fail before the raw tendon. Test it independently before powering the joint.

## Compare three methods

1. **Capstan wrap plus clamped tail:** three or more wraps around a smooth wooden drum, with the unloaded tail clamped between bolted plywood plates.
2. **Figure-eight around two standard bolts:** simple and adjustable, but creates small bend radii and local abrasion.
3. **Purchased low-profile rope terminal:** highest repeatability, but adds cost and metal.

## Test fixture

- Two guarded anchor blocks separated by an adjustable screw or come-along.
- Inline calibrated load cell.
- At least 300 mm free tendon length.
- Transparent impact shield and remote loading.
- Witness marks on the tail, clamp, and anchor.

## Procedure

1. Prepare five specimens per termination method using identical tendon batches.
2. Preload each specimen to 50 N for one minute and reset displacement zero.
3. Increase load in 50 N increments, holding each for 30 seconds.
4. Record slip, permanent elongation, strand damage, clamp deformation, and peak load.
5. Continue to controlled failure behind guarding.
6. Repeat the preferred method after 100 bend cycles around the intended pulley diameter.

## Initial acceptance target

- No measurable slip below 600 N.
- Predictable, contained failure above the target.
- No sharp metal edge contacting the tendon.
- Tool-assisted replacement and retensioning in under ten minutes.
- Less than 1 mm preload drift during a one-hour hold at 300 N.

The 600 N threshold is approximately twice the current 5 kg proof-load tendon force. Revise it when the design load changes.
