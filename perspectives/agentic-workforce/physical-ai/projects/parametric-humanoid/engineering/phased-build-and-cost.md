# Best Path Forward: Phased Build

## Phase A — Transmission truth

Build J1, termination coupons and an actuator dynamometer. Close G1–G4 before selecting full-body motors. Planning spend: under USD 1,500 including instrumentation not already available.

## Phase B — Load-bearing truth

Build J2 H-class joint, one foot/ankle rig and one gripper/rung rig. Close G5–G7. Planning cumulative robot-specific spend: USD 3,500–5,000.

## Phase C — Suspended lower body

Build pelvis, two legs, feet, battery/power bench and real-time lower-body control. Do not fabricate arms or decorative shells yet. Close G8–G10 through level stepping and stairs. Planning cumulative robot-specific spend: USD 7,000–9,000.

## Phase D — Full upper body

Add waist, torso, arms, hands, head, local brain and removable sculptural ribs. Planning cumulative robot-specific spend: approximately USD 12,600 before value engineering.

## Phase E — Ladder research decision

Proceed only if stairs are repeatable, power-loss behavior is controlled, rung hooks retain mechanically, and the safety case remains credible. Ladder capability is a research option, not a release commitment.

## Cost decision

The current planning total is USD 600 above the USD 12,000 target but below the USD 20,000 redesign ceiling. Do not reduce bearings, safety hardware, output sensing or fall arrest to close this gap. Seek savings through fewer actuator variants, common electronics, plywood nesting, reused test instrumentation and evidence-based elimination of unnecessary cosmetic parts.
