# Plywood Fabrication Standard

## Material control

- Use void-free birch plywood or an approved equivalent for load-bearing parts.
- Record supplier, nominal and measured thickness, sheet ID, moisture condition and visible defects.
- Reject sheets with core voids at joint regions, delamination, warping, water damage or inconsistent thickness outside the drawing allowance.
- Orient face grain along the dominant link load where practical; rotate laminated layers when multidirectional strength is required.

## Laser process

- Cut a kerf/fit coupon for every material batch and machine setup.
- Record machine, lens, focus, power, speed, passes, air assist and measured kerf.
- Remove char from bearing, tendon and bonded surfaces without changing critical dimensions.
- Do not release production cuts until a first-article part matches the corrected drawing.
- Etch part number, revision, sheet ID and orientation on every structural part.

## Geometry rules

- Bolt-hole center to free-edge distance: target ≥3× bolt diameter unless tested otherwise.
- Material between a bearing pocket and free edge: target ≥12 mm for J1; scale by load class.
- Internal slot corners receive adequate radius; avoid sharp reentrant corners in load paths.
- Tendon path bend radius is never below the tested minimum.
- Provide wrench access and captive/retained hardware where loss would be hazardous.
- Slotted holes may accommodate wood movement only where they do not compromise joint registration.

## Assembly rules

- Prefer through-bolts, large washers and locknuts for structural joints.
- Threaded inserts require pull-out coupon evidence and are not the sole retention for hazardous loads.
- Use witness marks on structural fasteners and tendon adjusters.
- Apply finish/sealant consistently after dimensional verification; keep finish out of bearing and electrical contact interfaces.
- Document adhesive type, mix, batch, cure, surface preparation and clamp pressure where bonding is used.

## Release levels

- `CONCEPT`: proportions only.
- `TEST`: guarded component/subsystem prototype.
- `FLIGHT`: not used; Lamina has no certified operational release.
- `FULL-BODY TEST`: configuration approved only for the named facility, tether, limits and procedure.
