# Full-System Assembly Sequence

1. **Incoming inspection:** plywood, shafts, bearings, fasteners, actuators, battery and electronics.
2. **Coupons:** kerf/fit, fastener pull-out, bearing retention, tendon termination and finish compatibility.
3. **Joint rigs:** complete J1 M-class and J2 H-class gates.
4. **Feet and ankles:** assemble force sensors, soles, hard stops and output sensing.
5. **Lower limbs:** build left/right mirrored thigh and shank modules; verify mass and center of mass.
6. **Pelvis:** install motor banks, power branches, service disconnect and lower-body controllers.
7. **Suspended lower body:** commission each axis, then double-support squat and stepping under gantry.
8. **Torso/waist:** install spine, waist joints, battery enclosure, brain computer and safety chain.
9. **Arms:** build and bench-test each arm before attachment.
10. **Hands:** validate object and rung retention, including power-loss behavior.
11. **Head/sensors:** install cameras and microphones after high-energy commissioning work.
12. **Body shells:** install decorative ribs only after all motion clearances and service access are approved.
13. **System commissioning:** configuration check, axis direction, limits, encoder plausibility, force/current/temperature limits and logging.
14. **Mobility progression:** suspended squat → center-of-mass transfer → stepping → guarded level walking → stairs → ladder fixture.

At every stage, update actual mass, center of mass, current draw, thermal data and maintenance findings in the engineering workbook.
