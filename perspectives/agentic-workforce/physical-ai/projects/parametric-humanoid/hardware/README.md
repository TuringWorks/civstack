## Current design assumptions

- Laser-cut plywood supplies the torso, pelvis, limb torsion boxes, feet, joint cheeks, gussets, covers, and replaceable plates.
- No custom metal machining is assumed.
- Motors, shafts, bearings, shoulder bolts, springs, fasteners, batteries, wiring, and electronics are purchased standard components.
- The primary latching E-stop is mounted on the fixed upper-back crossmember at shoulder height.
- The overhead fall-arrest tether uses a separate structural anchor and cannot obstruct the E-stop.
- Final actuator ratings, battery size, tendon diameter, bearing sizes, shaft diameters, and plywood thicknesses require measured load cases and prototype testing.

## BOM maturity

The files are planning BOMs, not purchase orders. Manufacturer part numbers and final quantities should be frozen only after the single-joint test rig establishes torque, temperature, backlash, tendon life, and duty-cycle requirements.
