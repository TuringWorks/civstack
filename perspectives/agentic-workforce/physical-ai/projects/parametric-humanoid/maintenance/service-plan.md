# Maintenance and Service Plan

## Before every energized session

- Inspect plywood cracks, delamination, moisture, bearing migration and impact marks.
- Check tendon fuzzing, flattening, tension, termination witness marks and pulley tracking.
- Check structural fastener witness marks and shaft collars.
- Exercise E-stop, pendant, service disconnect and contactor feedback.
- Verify encoder zero, limits, foot/hand contact sensors, IMUs and configuration hash.
- Inspect tether, anchor and exclusion-zone setup.

## Initial service intervals

| Item | Initial interval | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Tendons | Every 2 operating hours or after any overload | Inspect, measure preload and replace on damage |
| Structural fasteners | Every session for first 20 hours | Check witness marks and torque per approved procedure |
| Bearings/retainers | Every 10 hours | Inspect migration, play, noise and temperature |
| Plywood links | Every 10 hours and after impacts | Inspect and photograph critical regions |
| Battery/connectors | Every 10 charge cycles | Inspect temperature history, damage and connector condition |
| Calibration | Every session initially | Verify joint zero, feet, hands and IMUs |
| Full configuration | Before each test campaign | Archive software, limits, CAD and wiring hashes |

Intervals are provisional and must be replaced by evidence from endurance testing.

## Replace rather than repair when

- A structural wood part cracks, delaminates, shows bearing-seat movement or has been overloaded beyond its qualified envelope.
- A tendon shows broken/fuzzy strands, glazing, flattening, termination slip or unknown shock loading.
- A shaft is bent, scored or shows collar movement.
- A safety component has an uncertain history or fails a functional check.

## Spare strategy

Maintain matched left/right link blanks, joint cheeks, bearing retainers, sector/capstan laminations, tendon kits, standard bearings, shafts, fasteners, sensors, fuses and one spare controller per bus segment.
